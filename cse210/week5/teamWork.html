Overview

  • Task: As a team, write a program to play Jumper.
  • Purpose: Apply the principle of abstraction to the Jumper program.

    • Instructions

    1. Complete all of the activities in the team project below. This activity is to be completed as a team.
    2. This is a group submission, therefore, only one member of the group needs to submit the final assignment. All group members will receive the same score for the Team Challenge.

    Instructions

    1. Prepare yourselves to complete the team challenge. Use the basic project template to initialize it.
    2. Gather with your team and review the Jumper program requirements. Discuss the following questions during your meeting. Continue on when you've thoroughly addressed each one.
      • Jumper
      • How are the Hunter and Jumper games similar?
      • What classes should you use in your program?
      • What methods and attributes should each class have?
      • How will the classes work together in your program?
    3. Create a plan to complete the program. It's usually helpful to assign the programming tasks by classes.
    4. Program the Jumper game according to your plan. Be sure you use a branch-based workflow to integrate your invidivual work in a systematic way.

    Submitting

    All Members: Return to I-Learn and submit the corresponding assignment. Be sure to add the following information in your text entry when you submit. If you do not include all of the information you will not receive a grade for the assignment.

    • The URL for your team's remote repository
    • The grading category that best describes your team's work
    • One or two sentences justifying your choice
    The grading categories are as follows.
    • Some attempt made (1)
    • Developing but significantly deficient (2)
    • Slightly deficient (3)
    • Meets requirements (4)
    • Exceeds requirements (5)
